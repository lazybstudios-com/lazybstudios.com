Golden Days

A private journal for the memories, milestones, and quiet caregiving moments that families want to remember — and the trusted few they want to share with.

In Active Development

Overview

Golden Days gives families a calm, private space to record the parts of life worth holding onto — a child’s first words, a grandparent’s stories, an aging parent’s good-day routines, a sibling’s recovery progress — without surrendering any of it to a social network.

It’s built around a simple idea: the people who need to see a journal entry should see it. Nobody else.

What it’s for

  • Private journaling — daily notes, photos, and milestones kept in your own timeline.
  • Care notes & to-dos — medications, appointments, instructions, and routines shareable with the people doing the caregiving.
  • Milestones & memories — mark the first steps, the last good Sunday, the in-between days.
  • Searchable history — find a moment six months ago without scrolling through a year of photos.

The Circle of Caregivers

The signature feature of Golden Days. A primary account holder can build a Circle of trusted family members or caregivers and selectively share specific notes or to-dos with them. The shared adult child gets the “Dad ate well today, no nap needed” note; the visiting nurse gets the medication list and the morning routine; the daycare teacher gets the latest food allergy update.

Sharing is per-item, per-person, and revocable at any time. Nothing is shared by default. Circle members never see your full journal — only the entries you’ve explicitly invited them into.

Children & family privacy

Golden Days is directed at and used by adult caregivers, parents, and family members. While journals often record information about children, Golden Days does not provide accounts to minors and does not knowingly collect data directly from children. The adult account holder is solely responsible for the content of their journal entries. See the Privacy Policy for the full Children’s Privacy section.

Privacy & data

Journal entries, photos, and Circle membership are stored securely via Supabase, encrypted in transit and at rest, accessible only to the account holder and the specific people they’ve shared each item with. Export or permanently delete at any time through the data deletion portal.

In active development — private beta opening

Golden Days is being built with a small group of families. If you’d like to test early builds and help shape the Circle of Caregivers feature, request a demo or a beta invite.
