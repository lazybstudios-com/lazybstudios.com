Chemflo-Suite

An enterprise-grade platform for monitoring chemical process flow, managing inventory, and staying ahead of compliance audits.

Live & Deployed

Overview

Chemflo-Suite is currently in production with enterprise customers in regulated industrial operations. It consolidates real-time process telemetry, chemical inventory, batch records, and compliance documentation into a single centralized control plane — replacing the patchwork of spreadsheets, paper logs, and bespoke SCADA dashboards that most plants have grown up around.

Built specifically for environments where regulators, auditors, and operations all need to see the same numbers without anyone re-keying them.

Capabilities

  • Real-time process telemetry — flow, temperature, pressure, and reagent levels surfaced as actionable dashboards, not raw streams.
  • Inventory & reagent tracking — receive, stage, consume, and reconcile across batches with full audit trails.
  • Compliance & batch records — export-ready documentation for inspections, with versioning and electronic signatures.
  • Role-based access controls — floor operators, supervisors, QA, and management each see what they need and nothing they don’t.
  • Alerting & escalation — threshold breaches notify the right shift, the right way, on the right channel.
  • Centralized multi-site management — consolidate facilities under one tenant when operations span multiple plants.

Deployment & integration

Chemflo-Suite is offered as a managed cloud deployment with optional private-tenant and on-prem configurations for facilities with strict data-residency requirements. Standard integrations exist for common PLC and historian protocols; bespoke integration work is part of every onboarding.

Privacy & data

Enterprise data is stored in customer-isolated Supabase instances with row-level security policies and encrypted backups. Access is governed by your service agreement — we don’t use operational data for analytics or training, and we don’t share it across customers. See the Privacy Policy for general practices and your specific MSA for contractual specifics.
